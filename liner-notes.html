1 · Hold On Tight

"One of my favorite sounds is the Hammond B3 Organ. I love listening to guys like Jimmy Smith, Joey DeFrancesco, and Cory Henry. I wrote the shout chorus to this song in 2017 while experimenting with some traditional gospel chord changes and the rest materialized once The Guests came together and Cooper's voice took the tune to the heights I had hoped it could reach. I just wanted to get a song with an uplifting message out there." - David Buckley

5 · Avenue L

"The inspiration for this tune comes from my time living on the far-south side of Chicago called, interestingly enough, the 'East Side.' The roads in the East Side, and most of Chicago, are an intricate weave of one-way streets that can be confusing for newcomers and visitors. The trains and the soaring interstate speedway nicknamed 'the skyway' only add to the confusion for unfamiliar travellers. Nevertheless, there are quirks that make the madness beautiful: the series of drawbridges, the aging drydocks, the McDonald's in the middle of a six-lane freeway. I tried to capture some of that beautiful madness here." - Robert Alger

2 · Stay Awhile

"The Guests came together in February 2018 when Spencer was asked to get a group together for a friend's mocktail party. We had so much fun playing together and we just had to keep it going. A few months in, I had this song written out on keys and Aaron came over to my house and came up with the horn parts to fill in the sonic gaps. When he showed me the horn break he put together for the end of the tune I was floored. Weeks later, we performed this as our first original song." - David Buckley

4 · Consider Myself Packed

"Nowadays, I'm awake a little earlier because I have a young son. Sometimes while he's playing at 6 am I'll pull out my acoustic guitar and pluck out some harmonies. I love Steely Dan and the inverted chords in the verse of this song were my best attempt on one of those early mornings to sound like Fagen and Becker. Once I had that worked out, I recalled a chord progression I wrote way back in 2014 and knew that it would fit perfectly in the chorus. The process was a reminder to retain musical ideas even if they don't make a full track yet, there will be a spot for them down the road." - David Buckley
